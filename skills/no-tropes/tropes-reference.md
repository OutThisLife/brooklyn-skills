# AI Writing Tropes Reference

Source: [tropes.fyi](https://tropes.fyi) by [ossama.is](https://ossama.is)

## Word Choice

### "Quietly" and Other Magic Adverbs

Overuse of "quietly", "deeply", "fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably" to convey subtle importance.

- "quietly orchestrating workflows, decisions, and interactions"
- "the one that quietly suffocates everything else"
- "a quiet intelligence behind it"

### "Delve" and Friends

"Delve", "certainly", "utilize", "leverage" (as verb), "robust", "streamline", "harness".

- "Let's delve into the details..."
- "We certainly need to leverage these robust frameworks..."

### "Tapestry" and "Landscape"

Ornate nouns where simpler words work. Also: "paradigm", "synergy", "ecosystem", "framework".

- "The rich tapestry of human experience..."
- "Navigating the complex landscape of modern AI..."

### The "Serves As" Dodge

Replacing "is" with "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents".

- "The building serves as a reminder of the city's heritage."
- "The station marks a pivotal moment in the evolution of regional transit."

## Sentence Structure

### Negative Parallelism

"It's not X — it's Y." The single most common AI writing tell. One per piece can work; ten is an insult. Includes "not because X, but because Y" and "The question isn't X. The question is Y."

- "It's not bold. It's backwards."
- "Half the bugs you chase aren't in your code. They're in your head."

### "Not X. Not Y. Just Z."

Dramatic countdown negating things before revealing the point.

- "Not a bug. Not a feature. A fundamental design flaw."
- "not recklessly, not completely, but enough"

### "The X? A Y."

Self-posed rhetorical questions answered immediately.

- "The result? Devastating."
- "The worst part? Nobody saw it coming."

### Anaphora Abuse

Same sentence opening repeated multiple times in succession.

- "They could expose... They could offer... They could provide... They could create..."

### Tricolon Abuse

Overuse of rule-of-three, often extended to four or five. One tricolon is elegant; three back-to-back is a pattern failure.

- "Products impress people; platforms empower them. Products solve problems; platforms create worlds."

### "It's Worth Noting"

Filler transitions: "It bears mentioning", "Importantly", "Interestingly", "Notably".

- "It's worth noting that this approach has limitations."
- "Importantly, we must consider the broader implications."

### Superficial Analyses

Tacking "-ing" phrases onto sentences for shallow analysis: "highlighting its importance", "reflecting broader trends", "contributing to the development of..."

- "contributing to the region's rich cultural heritage"
- "underscoring its role as a dynamic hub of activity and culture"

### False Ranges

"From X to Y" where X and Y aren't on any real scale.

- "From innovation to implementation to cultural transformation."
- "From problem-solving and tool-making to scientific discovery, artistic expression, and technological innovation."

## Paragraph Structure

### Short Punchy Fragments

Excessive short sentences/fragments as standalone paragraphs for manufactured emphasis.

- "He published this. Openly. In a book. As a priest."
- "Platforms do."

### Listicle in a Trench Coat

Numbered points disguised as prose: "The first... The second... The third..."

- "The first wall is the absence of... The second wall is the lack of... The third wall is..."

## Tone

### "Here's the Kicker"

False suspense: "Here's the thing", "Here's where it gets interesting", "Here's what most people miss", "Here's the deal".

### "Think of It As..."

Patronizing analogies. Teacher mode assuming the reader needs metaphors.

- "Think of it like a highway system for data."
- "Think of it as a Swiss Army knife for your workflow."

### "Imagine a World Where..."

Futurism invitations to sell an argument.

- "Imagine a world where every tool you use has a quiet intelligence behind it..."

### False Vulnerability

Simulated self-awareness that reads as performative. Real vulnerability is specific and uncomfortable; AI vulnerability is polished and risk-free.

- "And yes, I'm openly in love with the platform model"
- "This is not a rant; it's a diagnosis"

### "The Truth Is Simple"

Asserting something is obvious instead of proving it. Also the dramatic reveal: "but none of them is the real story. The real story is..."

- "The reality is simpler and less flattering"
- "History is unambiguous on this point"

### Grandiose Stakes Inflation

Everything becomes world-historically significant. API pricing → fate of civilization.

- "This will fundamentally reshape how we think about everything."
- "will define the next era of computing"

### "Let's Break This Down"

Pedagogical voice assuming hand-holding. "Let's unpack this", "Let's explore", "Let's dive in".

### Vague Attributions

Unnamed "experts", "observers", "industry reports" without specifics.

- "Experts argue that this approach has significant drawbacks."
- "Industry reports suggest that adoption is accelerating."

### Invented Concept Labels

Compound labels that sound analytical without being grounded: "supervision paradox", "acceleration trap", "workload creep". Name a thing, skip the argument.

## Formatting

### Em-Dash Addiction

Limit to 2-3 per piece. AI uses 20+.

- "The problem — and this is the part nobody talks about — is systemic."

### Bold-First Bullets

Every bullet starts with a bolded phrase. Almost nobody formats lists this way by hand.

- "**Security**: Environment-based configuration with..."
- "**Performance**: Lazy loading of expensive resources..."

### Unicode Decoration

Unicode arrows (→), smart quotes, special characters not easily typed on a keyboard.

- "Input → Processing → Output"

## Composition

### Fractal Summaries

"What I'm going to tell you; what I'm telling you; what I just told you" at every level.

- "In this section, we'll explore... [3000 words later] ...as we've seen in this section."

### The Dead Metaphor

One metaphor beaten into the ground across the entire piece. Introduce, use, move on.

### Historical Analogy Stacking

Rapid-fire historical companies/tech revolutions for false authority.

- "Apple didn't build Uber. Facebook didn't build Spotify. Stripe didn't build Shopify."
- "Every major technological shift — the web, mobile, social, cloud — followed the same pattern."

### One-Point Dilution

Single argument restated 10 ways across thousands of words. 800-word argument padded to 4000 words of circular repetition.

### Content Duplication

Repeating sections verbatim. Happens when the model loses track of what it already wrote.

### The Signposted Conclusion

"In conclusion", "To sum up", "In summary". If you have to announce you're concluding, the writing isn't doing its job.

### "Despite Its Challenges..."

Rigid formula: acknowledge problems, immediately dismiss them. "Despite these challenges, the initiative continues to thrive."
